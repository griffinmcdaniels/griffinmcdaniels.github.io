Griffin McDaniels

Doctoral Academy Fellow at the Univ. of Arkansas

I am a PhD student at the University of Arkansas focusing on biodiversity and conservation genetics. My broad reserach interests include phylogenetics, phylogeography, population ecology, and evolution on the species level. I also am commited to outreach and making science more accessible and inviting.

I am currently a member of the Arkansas Convervation and Molecular Ecology lab, which focuses on bioinformatics and the 'omics and their applications in biodiversity assessment and management.

I find that reptiles and amphibians are excellent organisms for the studies of population genetics and lineage evolution and are additionally often unerappreciated in terms of biodiversity and public opinion. Therefore, I aim to produce research that expands knowledge (both general and actionable) and appreciation of these fascinating biodiversity assets.
